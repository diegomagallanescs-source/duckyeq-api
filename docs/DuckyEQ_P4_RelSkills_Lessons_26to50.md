**Level 26 · How to Fix a Broken Friendship**

*Tier 3 · Deeper Relationship Skills*

+:---------------------------------------------------------------------:+
| **Core Message --- duck highlights on lesson open**                   |
|                                                                       |
| **Most broken friendships can be fixed. It takes courage and honesty  |
| --- but it is almost always worth trying.**                           |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

**Objective:** Learn the steps to repair a friendship after something
went wrong --- and find the courage to go first.

**Quack of the Day**

*\[curious\] Why did the duck walk back across the whole pond to fix a
friendship he had let drift for months?*

*\[playful\] **Because he finally realized that \'it has been too long
now\' is something people tell themselves --- not something the other
person actually believes!***

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **CONNECT** 5 questions · duck is LARGE on screen · answers never
  stored

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

- Is there a friendship in your life that broke or drifted that you
  miss?

- Have you ever repaired a friendship after something went wrong? How
  did it happen?

- Has anyone ever come back to fix things with you after a long time?
  How did that feel?

- Random: if you could send a message right now to any friend you have
  lost touch with, who would it be?

- What usually stops people from being the one to go first?

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **DEFINE** concept + tappable flashcards · duck is SMALL in corner

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Concept:** Most broken friendships can be repaired. The first step is
going back and being honest. You say what you did, you say you are
sorry, and you ask if you can try again. That is all it takes to open
the door.

*Flashcards (tap-to-advance, duck expression per card):*

**1.** *\[curious\] \"What a real repair sounds like: \'I know things
have been weird between us. I miss you and I think I owe you an apology.
Can we talk?\'\"*

**2.** *\[thoughtful\] \"You do not have to have a perfect speech. You
just have to show up honestly. Most people are waiting for someone to go
first.\"*

**3.** *\[playful\] \"I once went three months not talking to a duck
over something neither of us could even really explain anymore. I
finally just said \'this is silly, I miss swimming with you.\' Done.\"*

**4.** *\[calm\] \"It is not too late. Even if it has been a long time.
Even if things got messy. Going back takes courage --- and it is almost
always welcome.\"*

**5.** *\[happy\] \"You do not have to fix everything in one
conversation. You just have to open the door. One honest conversation is
enough to start.\"*

**6.** *\[surprised\] \"Research shows most people in broken friendships
want to repair it but are waiting for the other person to go first. Both
people wait. Nobody goes. Going first is the rare and brave thing.\"*

**7.** *\[encouraging\] \"Go first. The friendship you miss is probably
on the other side of one honest conversation.\"*

**Level 27 · Healthy vs. Unhealthy Friendships**

*Tier 3 · Deeper Relationship Skills*

+:---------------------------------------------------------------------:+
| **Core Message --- duck highlights on lesson open**                   |
|                                                                       |
| **Not every friendship is good for you. Knowing the difference        |
| between a healthy one and an unhealthy one could change your life.**  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

**Objective:** Learn the signs of healthy and unhealthy friendships ---
and trust yourself when something does not feel right.

**Quack of the Day**

*\[curious\] Why did the duck finally admit to himself that one of his
friendships was actually making him feel worse --- not better?*

*\[playful\] **Because he kept thinking something was wrong with HIM ---

  would they have?

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **DEFINE** concept + tappable flashcards · duck is SMALL in corner

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Concept:** A healthy friendship makes you feel safe, good about
yourself, and free to be who you are. An unhealthy one makes you feel
small, scared, or like you have to pretend. Knowing the signs is how you
protect yourself.

*Flashcards (tap-to-advance, duck expression per card):*

**1.** *\[curious\] \"Healthy friendship signs: you feel safe. You can

**3.** *\[playful\] \"A duck who calls your ideas dumb every time you
share them is not a duck having a bad day. That is a pattern. Patterns

**4.** *\[calm\] \"You are allowed to say: \'this friendship does not
feel good anymore.\' You do not need a big reason. Your feelings are
reason enough.\"*

**5.** *\[happy\] \"Healthy friendships feel warm and easy most of the
time. Not perfect --- all friendships have bumps --- but mostly warm and
safe.\"*

**6.** *\[surprised\] \"Research shows that unhealthy close friendships
can have the same negative effects on mental health as bullying from
strangers. The relationship matters.\"*

**7.** *\[encouraging\] \"You deserve friendships that make you feel
good. You do not have to keep one that does not.\"*

**Level 28 · Setting Limits --- What You Are and Are Not Okay With**

*Tier 3 · Deeper Relationship Skills*

+:---------------------------------------------------------------------:+
| **Core Message --- duck highlights on lesson open**                   |
|                                                                       |
| **Knowing what you are okay with and what you are not --- and being   |
| able to say it --- is one of the most important skills you will ever  |
| learn.**                                                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

**Objective:** Learn what limits (boundaries) are, why they make
relationships healthier, and how to set them kindly but clearly.

**Quack of the Day**

*\[curious\] Why did the duck feel so much better in his friendships
after he started saying what he was and was not okay with?*

*\[playful\] **Because people cannot respect a line they do not know is
there --- and once the duck drew the line, everyone knew where to
stand!***

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **CONNECT** 5 questions · duck is LARGE on screen · answers never
  stored

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

- Is there something people do that bothers you, but you have never said
  anything about it?

- Has anyone ever set a limit with you? How did you react?

- Do you find it easy or hard to say \'I\'m not okay with that\'?

- Random: if you had a sign on your door that told people one rule
  before they came in, what would it say?

- Why do you think it is so hard for people to say what they are and are
  not okay with?

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **DEFINE** concept + tappable flashcards · duck is SMALL in corner

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Concept:** A limit (or boundary) is knowing what you are okay with and
being able to say it. It is not a wall. It is just being honest about
what works for you. Good friendships have room for both people\'s
limits.

*Flashcards (tap-to-advance, duck expression per card):*

**1.** *\[curious\] \"A limit is not a punishment. It is information.
You are telling someone: here is what works for me and what does not.
That is honest and kind.\"*

**2.** *\[thoughtful\] \"Setting a limit sounds like: \'I need some
quiet time before I can talk.\' Or \'Please don\'t share what I tell
you.\' Short, calm, and clear.\"*

**3.** *\[playful\] \"I once let a duck borrow things and never ask ---
because I never said anything. The moment I said \'can you ask me
first,\' he did. Every time. He just needed to know.\"*

**4.** *\[calm\] \"You do not have to be angry to set a limit. Calm and
clear works better than frustrated and loud. Say it before you get upset
if you can.\"*

**5.** *\[happy\] \"When you set a limit and the other person respects
it --- that is a sign of a healthy friendship. They care about what
matters to you.\"*

**6.** *\[surprised\] \"Research shows that people who can set and
respect limits in friendships report stronger, longer-lasting
relationships than those who cannot.\"*

**7.** *\[encouraging\] \"Your limits are yours. They are not too much.
The right people will respect them.\"*

**6.** *\[surprised\] \"Research on social networks shows that most

**4.** *\[calm\] \"Your worth is not decided by whether you got included
in one thing. One moment of feeling left out is painful --- but it does
not define you or your friendships.\"*

**5.** *\[happy\] \"Sometimes feeling left out is a signal: this group
might not be your people. That is useful information, not a life
sentence.\"*

**6.** *\[surprised\] \"Research shows that social exclusion activates
the same brain regions as physical pain --- which is why it hurts so
much. Your feelings are real and they make sense.\"*

**7.** *\[encouraging\] \"You are allowed to feel it. And then you get
to decide what you do with it. That part is yours.\"*
