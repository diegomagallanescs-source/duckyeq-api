**Pillar Definition**

Being safe, kind, respectful --- and showing up when it matters.

Pillar 4 covers the practical skills of healthy relationships: how to
treat people (The Golden Rule), how to listen, how to show up when
things are hard, how to work as a team, how to be the kind of friend a
dog is, and how to honestly audit your own friendship habits. These
lessons use clear, simple language --- 4th grade reading level
throughout.

**Lesson Map --- Your Notes Coverage**

- L2: The Golden Rule --- treat people the way YOU want to be treated,
  even when they\'re not kind to you

- L3: Be a Listener --- be someone who really listens to others

- L4: Be Like a Dog --- the dog analogy for showing up fully (excited,
  no judgment, fully present, playful, stays to the end)

- L6: Show Up When Things Are Hard --- being there during the hard
  moments in a relationship

- L9: The Good Friend Audit --- do you check in? do you listen? do you
  show up during bad times?

- L10: Relationships Are Teamwork --- facing problems together, not
  fighting each other

Remaining 19 lessons fill the pillar with research-backed and
CASEL-aligned content: saying sorry, building trust, making new friends,
peer pressure, saying no, jealousy, sportsmanship, forgiving, online
kindness, handling unkindness, including others, and the Tier 1/2
synthesis.

**Tier 1 · Foundations (Levels 1--10)**

The core relationship skills every person needs: what they are, the
Golden Rule, listening, dog energy, saying things kindly, showing up,
saying sorry, friendships as work, the Good Friend Audit, and teamwork.
