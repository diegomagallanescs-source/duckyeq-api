**Pillar 5 --- Where All Five Skills Converge**

Responsible Decision-Making is the capstone competency of the CASEL
framework --- the skill that puts all other EQ abilities into action.
You cannot make a truly responsible decision without self-awareness
(knowing what you feel and value), self-management (staying calm enough
to think clearly), social awareness (genuinely considering others), and
relationship skills (caring about how your choices land). This pillar
unifies all four.

**Concept:** The Stoplight Method is a three-step tool for any decision.
RED: stop and breathe --- do not act yet. YELLOW: think it through ---
is it safe, kind, and respectful? GREEN: act with intention --- you
decided, now go. In order. Every time.

ask three questions: Is this safe --- for me and for others? Is it kind?
Is it respectful? If yes to all three --- green.\"*

decided intentionally. You thought it through and you chose. That is
what responsible looks like --- even if the outcome is hard.\"*

**4.** *\[calm\] \"You can do the whole stoplight in five seconds. That
is all it takes. Stop → think three questions → go. Five seconds. The
difference between reactive and responsible.\"*

**5.** *\[happy\] \"The more you practice it, the faster it gets. After
a while, the stoplight becomes automatic --- your brain runs it without
you having to think about it.\"*

**6.** *\[surprised\] \"Research on impulse control shows that inserting
