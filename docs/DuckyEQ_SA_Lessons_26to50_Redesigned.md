**Level 32 · Shame vs. Guilt --- They Are Not the Same**

*Tier 4 · Depth*

+:---------------------------------------------------------------------:+
| **Core Message --- duck highlights on lesson open**                   |
|                                                                       |
| **Guilt says \'I did something bad.\' Shame says \'I am bad.\' One    |
| helps you grow. The other just hurts.**                               |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

**Objective:** Learn the difference between guilt (useful) and shame
(not useful) --- and how to turn shame into something you can work with.

**Quack of the Day**

*\[curious\] Why did the duck feel terrible after splashing everyone,
but then actually fixed it?*

*\[playful\] **Because he focused on what he DID --- not on being a
terrible duck forever!***

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **CONNECT** 5 questions · duck is LARGE on screen · answers never
  stored

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

- When you do something wrong, what does the feeling that follows feel
  like --- more like \'I messed up\' or more like \'I\'m a mess\'?

- Have you ever felt so bad about something that it stopped you from
  fixing it?

- Is there something from your past that you still feel ashamed about
  --- even though it\'s long over?

- Random: who\'s someone you think handles mistakes really well? What do
  they do?

- What\'s the difference between forgiving yourself and making excuses
  for yourself?

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **DEFINE** concept + tappable flashcards · duck is SMALL in corner

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Concept:** Guilt focuses on an action: \'I did something that hurt
someone. I want to make it right.\' That\'s useful. Shame focuses on
your identity: \'I\'m a bad person. I\'m broken.\' Shame doesn\'t
motivate change --- it just shuts you down.

*Flashcards (tap-to-advance, duck expression per card):*

**1.** *\[curious\] \"Guilt = \'I did a bad thing.\' Shame = \'I am a
bad thing.\' Same situation, completely different internal
experience.\"*

**2.** *\[thoughtful\] \"Guilt motivates repair. It says: something
needs fixing, and I can fix it.\"*

**3.** *\[playful\] \"When I splashed a friend by accident, guilt said
\'go apologize.\' Shame would have said \'hide forever.\' Very different
advice.\"*

**4.** *\[calm\] \"Shame often shuts people down because it attacks the
self. When you feel like you\'re fundamentally broken, what\'s the point
of trying?\"*

**5.** *\[sympathetic\] \"A lot of shame comes from old messages ---
being told you were bad, not just that you did something bad. Those
messages were wrong.\"*

**6.** *\[happy\] \"You can take full responsibility for an action
